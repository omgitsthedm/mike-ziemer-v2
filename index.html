About Mike the full story

Concert + Festival Promoter

When it comes to the music industry, Mike Ziemer is a man of many hats! Starting in 2002, Ziemer began interviewing bands and reviewing albums for online magazines in order to get into shows for free and meet his favorite artists. After building up a large list of contacts, he was asked by a local band to manage them. The band's first request was for Ziemer to help them put together a concert in the suburbs, the rest was history.

In March 2004, Third String Productions was born at The Plano Centre where Ziemer would hold shows in multiple rooms from 2004-2009 with attendance ranging from 500 to 4,800. The Plano Centre was the birthplace of Unsilent Night and So What?! Music Fest.

After outgrowing the Plano Centre, Ziemer began booking live events across Texas and beyond, averaging as many as 400 events annually between multiple markets from 2021-2023.

In 2022, Third String Entertainment formed a strategic joint venture with Disco Donnie Presents, named Disco Punk, to further grow the So What?! Music Festival brand. 2023's festival was the largest in the festival's history bringing in over 22,000 people across 2 days in Dallas, TX.

Ziemer is also a partner in Furnace Fest, taking place annually in Birmingham, AL featuring a wave of bands primarily from the late 90s and early 2000s hardcore and post-hardcore scene.

Over the last 20 years, Ziemer has been instrumental in helping introduce and build artists through his festivals including A Day To Remember, Enter Shikari, Forever The Sickest Kids, Dance Gavin Dance, Beartooth, Of Mice & Men, Polyphia, and many more.

Other Ventures

Ziemer has also independently run his own record label, Third String Records since 2014. The label has released music for over a dozen bands with streams totaling over 20 million across all platforms.

He also founded the artist management company, Flip A Switch Music Group alongside his partners Luke Knipe and Matt Dunne. The management company roster currently includes songwriters, producers, engineers, and bands.

During 2020, while live music was on pause, Ziemer opened up his first recording studio, Flip A Switch Studios in his backyard of his Oak Cliff home. In 2021, he opened his second recording studio in Deep Ellum. The studios have attracted some of Texas' hottest acts including Bashfortheworld, Tay Money, Lil Twist, SVDDEN DEATH, Luna Luna, LilBootyCall, and many more.

Business Venturesfull portfolio

So What?! Music Festival

Since 2007, So What?! Music Festival has taken place 15 times in Texas including 10 spring editions, 2 fall editions, 1 Houston edition, and 2 summer editions. The "go to" festival for metal / hardcore / pop punk / emo fans.

Furnace Fest

Originally taking place from 2000-2003, Furnace Fest was revived in 2021 as a resurrection of the event. Taking place annually in Birmingham, AL featuring bands from the late 90s and early 2000s hardcore and post-hardcore scene.

Monster Mosh Music Festival

Originally taking place in 2008-2009, Monster Mosh regained popularity in 2022 when it returned as a mini-festival at Gilley's in Dallas. In 2023, the festival expanded to 2 days.

Restless Fest / Restless Shows

Founded in 2023 by Mike Ziemer, Restless Fest is a music festival with heavy emphasis on next-gen music.

Flip A Switch Studios

Founded in 2020, offering affordable recording services in Deep Ellum and Oak Cliff.

Flip A Switch Music Group
